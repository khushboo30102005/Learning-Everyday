## 1. What are Quartiles?

Quartiles are **positional measures** in statistics that divide a dataset into **four equal parts** after arranging the data in ascending order.

Each part contains **25% of the data**.

---

## 2. Types of Quartiles

### ðŸ”¹ First Quartile (Q1)
- Also called **Lower Quartile**
- Separates the **lowest 25%** of data from the rest
- Represents the 25th percentile

---

### ðŸ”¹ Second Quartile (Q2)
- Also called **Median**
- Divides the data into **two equal halves**
- Represents the 50th percentile

---

### ðŸ”¹ Third Quartile (Q3)
- Also called **Upper Quartile**
- Separates the **lowest 75%** of data from the top 25%
- Represents the 75th percentile

---

## 3. Quartiles Example

Given data (already sorted):  
`2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 16`

- **Q2 (Median)** = (8 + 10) / 2 = 9  
- **Q1** = Median of lower half (2,4,6,8) = (4 + 6) / 2 = 5  
- **Q3** = Median of upper half (10,12,14,16) = (12 + 14) / 2 = 13
