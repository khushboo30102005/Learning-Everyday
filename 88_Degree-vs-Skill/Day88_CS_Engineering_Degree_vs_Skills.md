# Day 88 | #100dayslearningchallenge  

## Do You REALLY Need a CS / Engineering Degree to Get into Big Tech?

### Short Answer: Mostly Yes.

---

## Reality of the Tech Job Market (CS & Engineering Context)

In most **Big Tech and IT job openings**, a **CS / Engineering Bachelor’s degree** is listed as a **minimum qualification**.  
This is especially true for roles like:

- Software Engineer  
- Backend Engineer  
- System Engineer  
- Data Engineer  
- Platform & Infrastructure Roles  

A degree often works as an **entry-level filter** during hiring.

---

## Are There Exceptions?

Yes — **exceptions do exist**.

If someone couldn’t complete a **CS / Engineering degree due to genuine reasons**, career growth is **still possible** —  
but only when **skills are truly extraordinary**.

---

## What Is Required Without a CS / Engineering Degree?

To compete without a formal degree, you must strongly compensate with:

- **Strong CS fundamentals**
  - Data Structures & Algorithms  
  - Operating Systems  
  - DBMS  
  - Computer Networks  

- **Meaningful, real-world projects**
  - Not just tutorials  
  - Projects that show problem-solving & scalability thinking  

- **Proof of work**
  - GitHub repositories  
  - Portfolio  
  - Contributions / deployments  

- **Excellent problem-solving ability**
  - Coding + system thinking  

---

## Degree vs Skills: The Truth

- **Degree = Entry Filter**
- **Skills = Growth Factor**

Big Tech companies don’t just hire degrees —  
they hire **engineers who can solve real problems**.

---

## Best Combination for Maximum Opportunities

> **CS / Engineering Degree + Strong Skills = Best Career Leverage**

---

## Final Thought

A degree may open the door,  
but **skills decide how far you go in the tech industry**.
